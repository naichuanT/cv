User Testing

Key findings

Landing page
In the first version of the design, we put a map as our landing page, but users reflect that it makes them confused about the main purpose of our apps. Thinking it is not about security, it is a navigation app.

Check-in page
We separate the check-in page and chat room in an early version of the design, but it makes the user feel confused what the difference between these two functions because both are navigating to the chatroom.

S.O.S
It is a button on the chatroom page, but we notice that it is not intuitive and won’t able to respond to the user immediately when the user needs help.

Check-in
Only check-in is not enough to cover in some situations because people won’t remember to check in again.

Fake call
It is not a bot at first. It is too obvious which might put the user in danger when they need a fake call to help.

User concern
Privacy issue of their location

Solutions

---
We change our main function page to be landing page, which is the check-in page.

---
We combine both chatroom and check-in page, and make the design of the page simpler. Giving users more options to convey their safety condition, so they don't need to spend too much time typing.

---
We change SOS button to an SOS shortcut, so users don’t need to open the app first and then call for help. And also change the default setting of calling out SOS to avoid complexity.

---
We add recurring check-in to help users to keep updating their safety conditions.

---
We put the fake call function inside the bot, making it looks like chatting with a real person.

---
We add loaction sharing time setting

Commercial

Feature

Check-in

This feature ensures that someone knows your current security situation, rather than asking for help when you have a real security problem. You can use this feature to report to as a group or as a single friend.

You can also choose what information to share and how long you want to it last. Recurring check-in is to ensure your safety, especially since people might not keep their attention on their phones all the time while they are out, but with this feature, your friends can still know your situation.

Fake call

When facing a new person or environment, if there is a fake call, it can effectively resolve some embarrassing or uneasy situations

For example, when we enter a relatively chaotic street, talking on the phone will make other people think that we are in contact with others, which will effectively reduce the probability of criminals targeting us.

Or when we have to meet with strangers, calling our fake call function by using the dialogue box, which can also reduce the other party's doubts and help us get out of the current situation.

Friends on Map

Using this app, we can not only ensure our own safety, but we can also reply to friends' check-in and ensure their safety by their location on the map with the crime rate.

Crime rate / Safe route

The crime rate filter in the map function is composed of police data. The route displayed on our map will be a relatively safe route for users.

For example, when we are going back to the parking lot to pick up the car at night, we can integrate recurring check-in functions with our safe route function. This can ensure that the user arrives at the pick-up place safely, and recurring check-in can avoid accidents and unpredictability happening on the road.
